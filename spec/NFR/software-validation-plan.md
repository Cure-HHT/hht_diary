Scope: Defines exactly what is being validated (e.g., the mobile app, cloud portal, and the Rave sync interface) and, just as importantly, what is not in scope.
Validation Strategy: Describes your approach (e.g., GAMP 5 risk-based validation). This is where you would document your "dogfooding" extraction tool and your Order of Precedence for definitions.
Roles & Responsibilities: A matrix (or DoA Log) defining who writes the tests, who executes them, and who has final approval authority.
Acceptance Criteria: The "definition of done." It states that 100% of critical requirements must pass and any minor bugs must be documented as "allowable deviations".
Deliverables: A checklist of the documents you will produce (e.g., Risk Assessment, Traceability Matrix, and VSR).

Why the SVP is the "Foundation"
Risk-Based Effort: The FDA encourages a "risk-based approach". The SVP explains why you are doing 50 tests for Audit Trails (high risk) but perhaps only 5 for the "Help" menu (low risk).
Audit Defense: If an auditor finds a discrepancy, they first look at the SVP to see if you followed your own plan. Failure to follow the SVP is often cited in FDA Warning Letters.
Standardization: For a vendor, the SVP ensures that your team uses the same "Requirement Style Guide" and "Test Protocol" format across the entire project.
